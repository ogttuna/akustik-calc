## Product Target

DynEcho must produce meaningful acoustic results for arbitrary realistic
wall and floor layer combinations.

The resolver order is:

1. exact measured same-topology, same-metric, same-basis row;
2. nearby measured similarity anchor only when route, topology, support
   family, metric basis, and owner fields are compatible;
3. calibrated family solver;
4. source-absent family solver with explicit error budget;
5. precise `needs_input` or `unsupported` boundary.

Source rows are exact overrides, anchors, calibration evidence,
holdouts, and regression tests. They are not the product by themselves.
The product is the resolver plus family solvers.

## INSUL / Standards Takeaways

Public INSUL documentation supports this plan:

- INSUL is a prediction calculator for walls, floors, roofs, ceilings,
  windows, impact sound, and rain noise. It evaluates material and
  construction changes rather than acting only as a finite catalog.
- Its public feature page says it predicts TL or `Ln` in 1/3 octave
  bands and derives `STC`, `Rw`, `IIC`, or `LnTw` from those results.
- Its technical page describes single-panel mass law, coincidence,
  thick-panel effects, finite-size low-frequency behavior, and
  double-panel regions for mass law, mass-air-mass decoupling, cavity
  coupling, and solid bridging.
- Its user guide says INSUL uses theoretical models needing limited
  input data, refined by comparison with lab tests, and that prediction
  is not a substitute for test data.
- Its floor-cover guide separates measured floor-cover improvement from
  predicted improvement, warns against transferring lightweight and
  heavyweight floor-cover evidence casually, and requires physical
  properties such as thickness, density, bending stiffness, damping, and
  dynamic stiffness for predicted underlay/floor-cover behavior.
- ISO 12354-1/2 keep field and building calculations tied to direct and
  flanking transmission, structural propagation, frequency-band
  calculations, and uncertainty. ISO 717-1/2 keep single-number ratings
  downstream of banded airborne or impact results.

DynEcho consequence: keep building field/basis boundaries strict, move
source-absent solvers toward internal banded curves, and use exact or
nearby measured rows only through compatible candidate selection.

### Design Payload Requirements

The formula corridor should not invent a universal layer formula. It
should define branch-specific design payloads from existing owned
families:

- bare carrier reference from raw-bare open-box/open-web formula lanes
  when the support is bare-compatible;
- lower-treatment improvement from ceiling mass, cavity depth,
  absorber, and suspension support class;
- branch penalty or adjustment for direct-fixed versus resilient lower
  support and short-circuit risk;
- explicit confidence and budget decomposition, not measured evidence.

Preferred formula expression for this gate:

```text
airborneRwDesign =
  bareCarrierRwReference
  + lowerBoardMassGain
  + cavityAbsorberGain

impactLnWDesign =
```

## Broader Generalization Program

After the helper-only lane closes, move from slice-by-slice corridors
toward a global resolver:

1. Reference benchmark registry.
   Normalize exact rows, holdouts, similarity anchors, and formula
   anchors across wall and floor.

2. Assembly fingerprint.
   Build route-specific fingerprints from support family, leaf/cavity
   topology, material class, thickness, mass, dynamic stiffness,
   spacing, lower/upper treatments, field/building context, and rating
   basis.

3. Similarity distance.
   Allow measured-row adjustment only when hard gates pass: same route,
   compatible topology, same metric basis, same support family, owned
   physical deltas, and visible correction terms.

4. Family solver registry.
   Each solver must declare required inputs, supported outputs, basis,
   formula terms, budget terms, positive fixtures, nearby negatives, and
   hostile-input behavior.

5. Banded internal curves.
   New source-absent solvers should produce or preserve banded curve
   provenance whenever the physics is spectral. Single-number `Rw`,
   `C`, `Ctr`, `Ln,w`, `CI`, and `CI,50-2500` should be adapters, not
   unexplained constants.

6. Weak-lane migration.
   Existing `low_confidence`, `screening_fallback`, and generic
   predictor lanes must be ranked as debt. Each either becomes exact,
   similarity-anchored, calibrated, source-absent-budgeted, or a precise
   unsupported boundary.
